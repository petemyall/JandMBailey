Day 1: From Heathrow to Hello Tokyo!

After months of planning, lists longer than a Shinkansen (🚄), and enough YouTube vlogs to qualify as “Japan experts,” we finally made it! Heathrow's Terminal 5 felt like a test of stamina: dodging queues, battling for a decent cup of tea, and snagging extra legroom seats like seasoned pros.

The flight? Smooth as silk (thank you, Japan Airlines) with a surprisingly edible chicken teriyaki and a binge of “Lost in Translation” to set the mood. Upon landing at Haneda Airport, we were greeted by Japan’s famous efficiency: customs was a breeze, and we were officially on Japanese soil in record time.

Jet lag? Oh yes. But adrenaline—and the promise of our first bowl of ramen—pushed us through. Our hotel in Asakusa is a blend of old and new, with tatami mats, a robotic check-in, and a bathroom panel that could launch a spaceship. Yes, we accidentally triggered the bidet. Twice.

We strolled to Sensoji Temple just as the lanterns glowed. The street food—taiyaki (fish-shaped cakes filled with red bean), yakitori (grilled skewers), and the sweet, smoky scent of freshly grilled mochi—lured us like moths to a neon flame.

After nearly 24 hours of travel and exploring, we collapsed into bed. Tomorrow? Sushi, sumo, and maybe a karaoke bar if we can keep our eyes open. Stay tuned!
